# Harvest Finance ($HARV) Tokenomics Model

This document defines the token economic model for Harvest Finance, ensuring long-term sustainability, incentive alignment, and fairness across all protocol participants.

## 1. Token Overview
- **Token Name:** Harvest DAO Token
- **Ticker:** `$HARV`
- **Total Maximum Supply:** 10,000,000 HARV (Hard-capped, no minting function post-deployment)
- **Token Standard:** ERC-20 (with ERC20Votes extension for governance)
- **Utility:** Governance voting, Yield boosting, Protocol fee distribution

---

## 2. Token Allocation

The total supply of 10,000,000 `$HARV` is allocated to balance community ownership with long-term protocol development and stability.

| Category | Percentage | Amount (HARV) | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Community & Liquidity Mining** | 45% | 4,500,000 | Rewards for Liquidity Providers (LPs) and Vault depositors. |
| **Treasury & DAO Fund** | 20% | 2,000,000 | Governed by the DAO for grants, audits, partnerships, and bug bounties. |
| **Core Contributors (Team)** | 20% | 2,000,000 | Incentivizes long-term commitment from the founding and core engineering team. |
| **Investors & Strategic Partners** | 10% | 1,000,000 | Capital for initial bootstrap, security audits, and early liquidity. |
| **Airdrop / Bootstrap** | 5% | 500,000 | Rewarding early testnet users and bootstrapping initial DAO governance. |

## 3. Vesting Schedule

To prevent supply shocks and align long-term incentives, strict vesting schedules are applied via Timelock/Vesting smart contracts.

- **Community & Liquidity Mining:** Emitted linearly over a **4-year period** via an emission curve that decays by 20% annually to ensure initial bootstrap traction without hyperinflation.
- **Treasury & DAO Fund:** 10% unlocked at TGE (Token Generation Event) to fund immediate operational needs; the remaining 90% is vested linearly over **3 years**.
- **Core Contributors (Team):** **6-month cliff**, followed by linear vesting over **36 months**. Prevents early dumping by the team.
- **Investors:** **6-month cliff**, followed by linear vesting over **24 months**.
- **Airdrop / Bootstrap:** 100% unlocked at TGE, but claiming requires interacting with the protocol (e.g., staking or depositing in a vault) to ensure active user retention.

## 4. Incentive Alignment & Value Accrual

The `$HARV` token is designed to capture protocol value and align the interests of users, token holders, and the DAO.

1. **Governance Rights:** `$HARV` is used to vote on protocol parameters, whitelist new vaults, adjust fee structures, and manage the Treasury via the `HarvestGovernor` contract.
2. **Yield Boosting (veTokenomics-lite):** Users who stake and lock their `$HARV` tokens receive a multiplier on their Vault yield farming rewards. This encourages long-term holding and reduces circulating supply velocity.
3. **Protocol Revenue Sharing:** A portion of the performance fees generated by Harvest Finance Vaults is sent to a Fee Distributor contract. The DAO can vote to use these funds to either:
   - Market Buy-back and burn `$HARV`.
   - Distribute stablecoins (e.g., USDC) to staked `$HARV` holders as dividends.

## 5. Economic Risk Analysis & Mitigations

### Risk 1: Hyperinflation and Token Devaluation
**Problem:** High early emission rates can dilute token value rapidly (the "farm and dump" problem).
**Mitigation:** The `$HARV` supply is hard-capped at 10 million. Liquidity mining emissions decay annually. Furthermore, the introduction of yield boosting requires farmers to hold/lock `$HARV` to maximize returns, creating a constant sink/demand for the token.

### Risk 2: Governance Capture by Whales or Early Investors
**Problem:** Concentrated token holdings could allow malicious actors to pass proposals that drain the treasury or change fee structures for personal gain.
**Mitigation:** 
- The `HarvestGovernor` requires a 4% quorum.
- A `HarvestTimelock` (minimum 24-48 hours) is enforced so users can exit the protocol if a malicious proposal passes.
- Team and Investor tokens do not have voting power while locked in the vesting contracts.

### Risk 3: Just-In-Time (JIT) Reward Exploitation
**Problem:** Flash loan attackers deposit massive capital right before a reward distribution to steal yield from long-term LPs.
**Mitigation:** The emission mechanism tracks the time-weighted average balance of depositors. Rewards are distributed block-by-block, making JIT flash loan dilution mathematically impossible. 

### Risk 4: Liquidity Mercenaries
**Problem:** Capital leaves the protocol once the native token emissions drop.
**Mitigation:** By transitioning protocol yield from pure `$HARV` emissions to Real Yield (sharing actual stablecoin revenue generated by Vault performance fees), the protocol remains attractive even after the 4-year emission schedule completes.
